Lessons Learned and Future Work 9

Challenges and Solutions:

  1.  Analyzing nearly 100 years of data (1918-2022) posed significant challenges in terms of data consistency, completeness, and the need for extensive data cleaning and normalization. 
  2.  Running Python code generated by Namrta within Microsoft Fabric proved challenging, as some code required modifications to ensure compatibility with the Fabric environment. 
  3.  Some DAX formulas needed adjustments to produce accurate results, requiring careful modifications to fit the analysis requirements. 

Lessons Learned:

  • Compatibility Considerations: When integrating different tools and platforms, such as Python code in Microsoft Fabric, it’s important to anticipate and address compatibility issues early to avoid implementation delays.
  • Flexibility in Code and Formulas: Code and formulas often need adjustments to meet specific project requirements. Being prepared to refine and tweak them as needed is crucial for achieving accurate results.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration: Effective communication and collaboration among team members across different departments (e.g., data science, engineering, and analysis) are vital for overcoming technical challenges and ensuring project success.
  • Scalability and Adaptability: Solutions should be designed with scalability in mind, allowing for adjustments and expansions as project requirements evolve or as more data becomes available.

Future Capabilities:

The future capabilities of this project could be transformative, focusing on several key areas of advancement. With the integration of more sophisticated machine learning models, predictive analytics can be enhanced to offer even more accurate forecasts, optimizing maintenance schedules and further reducing the risk of failures. The system could also be expanded to incorporate real-time data feeds from aircraft sensors and operational systems, enabling immediate insights and quicker decision-making. Enhanced automation through AI-driven tools could streamline maintenance and training processes, minimizing human error and boosting operational efficiency. Additionally, scalable solutions can be developed to ensure consistent safety and efficiency improvements across different aircraft fleets and operators. The project could also evolve to offer more customizable and interactive dashboards, allowing users to drill down into specific data points and trends, providing tailored insights. Furthermore, by integrating comprehensive risk management features that consider a broader range of factors, the project could further mitigate the likelihood of incidents. Finally, expanding the dataset to include global aviation data would enable robust comparisons, trend analysis, and the identification of international best practices, ensuring the project’s relevance and impact on a global scale.
