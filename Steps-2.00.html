Executive Summary 2

This project showcases how advanced analytics and predictive modeling were applied to aviation crash data to transform safety protocols. By analyzing historical flight data, key factors such as flight phase and crash causes were identified, enabling accurate prediction of crash probabilities. This data-driven approach helps aviation authorities and airlines address high-risk scenarios and enhance overall safety and risk management strategies.


Background

The aviation industry faces safety challenges, with technical failures responsible for around 25% of accidents globally. As aircraft complexity and traffic volume grow, there is a heightened need for advanced safety measures. This project uses predictive analytics, real-time monitoring, and data-driven insights to identify and mitigate potential technical failures, enhancing overall safety standards.


Problem Statement

Aviation safety remains a critical concern despite advancements in technology and safety protocols. 60-70% of all aviation accidents are caused by human error, making it the leading factor contributing to crashes. This includes errors in pilot judgment, communication failures, and procedural mistakes during key flight phases like takeoff and landing. Additionally, 17-20% of aircraft accidents are attributed to mechanical or technical failures, resulting from inadequate maintenance, unforeseen technical faults, or system malfunctions.


Objective 

To enhance aviation safety by reducing crashes caused by technical failures and human error by implementing predictive maintenance systems and enhancing pilot training programs, which will help minimize crash risks, reduce costs, and deliver a high return on investment (ROI).
