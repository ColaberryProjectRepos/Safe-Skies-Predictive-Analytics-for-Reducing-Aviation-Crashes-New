Visualizations: Case 1: Objective: Improve Aviation Safety by Reducing Technical Failure-Related Crashes 6

Positive Insight:

  • Reduction in Crashes During Takeoff:

  Since 1918, there have been 6,091 air crashes attributed to technical failures, including 1,724 occurring during takeoff, with a minimal year-over-year increase of 0.21%. Implementing predictive maintenance could reduce these takeoff crashes by 40%, significantly improving safety during this crucial phase and providing substantial benefits to operators.  

Negative Insight:

  • High Fatality Rate in Technical Failures:
    Technical failure-related crashes currently have a 4.9% fatality rate, resulting in around 30,000 fatalities or 5 per crash. Addressing these failures is crucial, as reducing them can significantly improve passenger safety.

ROI:
 Implementing predictive maintenance systems can save operators $5 million annually by avoiding repair costs, reducing compensation payouts, and lowering legal fees. With a $1 million investment, the net profit is $4 million, resulting in an ROI of 400%. This high ROI highlights the financial viability of investing in predictive maintenance systems.
